Project Goals

This project focused on evaluating and improving a virtual museum website to increase engagement and accessibility for culturally curious audiences.

The primary goals were to understand how similar institutions structure and present their digital offerings, assess usability issues in the existing site, and recommend improvements for clearer navigation, more intuitive content layout, and enhanced discoverability of virtual exhibitions and learning resources.

Ultimately, the goal was to lay a strong UX foundation for future expansion and ensure the site could scale as new exhibitions and educational materials were added.

Project Requirements

Clarify Site Navigation

Does the navigation help users intuitively find exhibitions, resources, and learning tools without confusion or backtracking?

Benchmark Against Industry Leaders

How do other virtual or hybrid museums structure their platforms, and what can we learn from their engagement strategies?

Design for Growth & Discoverability

Can the site design support future exhibitions and evolving content while maintaining a clean, user-friendly structure?

Address Accessibility & Usability Issues

What improvements can be made to ensure the site is accessible, engaging, and easy to use for diverse user groups?

User Research & Discovery

To explore how users engage with virtual museum content, We used a mixed-methods research approach that combined desk research, competitor analysis, surveys, and 1:1 Zoom interviews. This helped us ground the redesign in real user behavior and expectations.

Desk Research

I studied current UX trends in virtual museums, focusing on accessibility, content structure, and engagement. Reviewed published case studies and reports from cultural institutions and design firms.

Competitor Analysis

I analyzed navigation structures, interaction models, and exhibit entry flows from hybrid and fully virtual museums for strengths, friction points, and opportunities.

Surveys & Usability Interviews

All four team members collected data from survey participants and Team member (2) conducted usability interviews. I helped design the questionnaire and usability tasks, also Thematic Analysis of all data.

Insights and Design Suggestions

Core Insights from Competitor Analysis

Similar Website Layouts

They use similar layouts to provide access to and promote their virtual experiences.

Audience Targeting

Educational institutions are frequently targeted, suggesting a strong educational component in digital strategies.

Multiple Access Points

Digital/virtual experiences are made accessible through various links, pages, and sections, giving flexibility in how they interact.

Clear CTAs

Prominent calls to actions guide users from initial exposure to fully engaging with the virtual experiences.

Core Insights from Primary Research

Lack of Intuitive Navigation

The current navigation menu is not user-friendly, and IA is confusing, making it difficult to find relevant content.

Outdated Design Elements

The website has outdated design layouts and elements, affecting engagement.

Unclear CTAs and Interactions

Users struggled to identify the available features and calls to action (CTAs) on the site.

Information Overload

The site presents too much information, leading to difficulty locating key content.

Design Suggestions

Restructure Site Map & Navigation

Restructuring the Site Map and Navigation Menu for clearer, more intuitive navigation.

Optimize Content Layout

Optimizing the Content Layout/Structure to ensure content is more accessible and well-organized.

Establish Design System & Guidelines

Establishing Style Guidelines & Design System to create consistent design elements and a cohesive visual language that aligns with the museum’s brand.

Conclusion: Thoughts and Takeaways

The goal of this project was to redesign the user experience for a virtual museum, enhancing navigation and accessibility to create a more engaging and intuitive online experience for users. While the actual UI designs are covered by an NDA and cannot be shared, the research insights and design suggestions played a pivotal role in shaping the UI design process.

Key Takeaway: Research-Driven Design

This project emphasized the importance of a research-driven approach to design, which allowed us to identify key usability issues and pain points for users. By focusing on competitor analysis, user interviews, and observational research, we provided valuable insights that informed the design suggestions for better content structure, navigation, and CTA placement.
