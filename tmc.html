UX Research

Objective

This project aimed to improve the discoverability of content and wayfinding across the Textile Museum of Canada’s website. Therefore, our research focused on understanding user needs and behaviors related to how visitors find and navigate information on the site.

Activities & Methods

Content Inventory

Audited existing pages and menu items to identify redundancies and unclear labeling.

Card Sorting & Tree Testing

Conducted card sorting with users to understand their mental models. Used tree testing (Optimal Workshop) to validate navigation paths.

Competitive Benchmarking

Reviewed navigation and IA patterns from leading museum and arts websites.

Key Findings

Overlapping categories and vague menu labels
Struggled to locate subpages
Hamburger menu obscured navigation
Accessibility issues with color contrast

Design Touchpoints & Decisions

I realized that changing both the IA and the navigation menu design at once would make it difficult to isolate the impact of each. To ensure we could measure the effect of the new IA independently, I proposed a phased implementation and testing strategy:

Phase 1: Test New IA in Existing Menu

Integrate the new, consolidated IA into the current menu structure with minimal visual changes.

Gather user feedback and analytics to assess improvements in navigation and content discoverability.

➡
Phase 2: Test New Navigation Menu Design

Move away from the hamburger menu to a visible, mega menu-styled navigation bar.

Gather user feedback and analytics to assess improvements in navigation and content discoverability.

➡
Rationale behind Two Phase Strategy

Clearly attribute improvements to the correct intervention.

Reduce disruption to real users.

Ensure Changes are Real-time Data-Driven.

What I Learned

The goal of this project was to create a clear, accessible user experience that makes it easier for people to discover and navigate the Textile Museum of Canada’s rich collections, exhibitions, and events.

This project also strengthened my ability to prioritize features and content, focusing on what would have the most impact for both first-time visitors and returning patrons. I found that building trust online comes from making the digital experience simple and transparent.

Key Takeaway: Blending User Needs with Post-deployment Business Goals

The most valuable insight was learning to balance immediate user needs such as content discoverability with the museum’s long-term goals, such as increasing engagement and supporting their growth. A phased, research-driven plan ensured both were addressed.
