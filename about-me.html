Who am I as a designer?

I’m the kind of designer who will ask, “Why does this need to exist?” before deciding how to build it. I collaborate easily with researchers, editors, developers, and subject matter experts. My goal is always to make things easier to use, easier to understand, and easier to maintain. I’ve worked in remote teams, start up teams, small businesses, and academic environments. In all of them, I aim to bring a calm to the table.

What technical experience do I have?

I work across the full product design process, from early research to final UI handoff. I use tools like Figma, FigJam, and Miro for designing and collaborating, and I’m comfortable creating responsive layouts, UI kits, and systems that scale. My background includes designing for both web and mobile, always keeping accessibility and usability at the core.

On the research side, I run interviews, build surveys, map user journeys, and synthesize findings into actionable insights. I’ve done moderated usability tests, light A/B testing, and workshop facilitation for internal teams.

I collaborate closely with developers using annotated specs, basic front-end familiarity (HTML/CSS), and sprint-based workflows. My presentations are clear and grounded, shaped by client decks, team critiques, and stakeholder reviews.

My toolkit includes Figma, Adobe Creative Suite, WordPress, Framer, Notion, Maze, Lookback, and Google Analytics (certified). I’m adaptable, and I choose the right tools for the job rather than relying on buzzwords.
