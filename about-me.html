Service Design About Me

Designing vivid and usable digital experiences rooted in research, systems thinking, and a passion for storytelling.

From engineering faculty websites to film pitch decks, I’ve led product and service design projects across education, media, fintech, and healthcare.

I currently maintain the ECE Department website at the University of Toronto and previously shaped production processes at Fix It In Post Studios, while also freelancing in design, research, and digital strategy.

Based in Toronto and originally from Chennai, I hold a Master’s in User Experience Design from the University of Toronto and a Master’s in Media from Anna University.

Since 2019, I’ve worked on agency and freelance projects focused on improving experiences across creative and technical domains.

Feel free to reach out — I’m chronically online.

My Design Process

Step 1

UX & Market Research

I begin with the goals, audience, and context. Through user interviews, competitive analysis, and assessing business needs, I gather insights that influence my direction.

Step 2

UI: Ideate & Brainstorm

I brainstorm a variety of solutions, sketching ideas, and creating wireframes on Figma. I also quickly whip up prototypes and concepts, if need be.

Step 3

UI: Wireframes & Prototype

Once I have a clear direction and I receive my go-ahead, I develop high-fidelity mockups. At this stage, I focus on working with both aesthetics and usability.

Step 4

Test, Iterate, Present

I conduct testing, gather feedback by presenting it to peers and stakeholders, and then fine-tune the final design. I am very flexible with stakeholder feedback, making designs that satisfy business goals as well as consumer/user needs.

Additional Step

Post-Design

If required, I am able to create content using Adobe Suite, Canva and AI, as well as use front end web development and software such as Wix, WordPress, HTML and CSS; as well as maintain and update the digital products.
