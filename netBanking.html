Challenge

First-time netbanking users, used to UPI/Google Pay's quick transactions, encountered absent beneficiary activation steps, authentication delays, and no real-time instruction. The legacy browser UI disguised critical information regarding this aspect of transfering funds; there were no tooltips, FAQs, or in-context teaching.

The above image, a screengrab from the application was how the transfer options were presented. There were a note and a scrolling flash banner below this but it was regarding fraudlent transactions and a warning to the user to link their Aadhar (India's Standarized Identification card) to their account. On a hilarious note, there is a literal ad for their credit card with an offer for availing a discount through a food delivery app at the top.

Current User Flow:

1. User clicks “Transfer Funds.”
2.Picks a transfer option, but discovers midway a beneficiary isn’t added.
3. Has to quit, navigate to an obscure part of the sidebar, add a beneficiary, enter OTP, then search in a separate menu to check activation status.
4. Waits (without visible countdown or guidance) for limits to unlock before retrying the transfer.

Security Constraints in Beneficiary Management:

1.If you add a new beneficiary, you must authenticate with OTP.
2. Small transfers (often up to ₹50,000) can sometimes be done immediately after activation.
3. Full transfer limits are enabled only after a waiting period (cooling period): usually 30 minutes to 24 hours depending on transaction type and account.
4. Trying to transfer to a beneficiary who isn’t “Active” always fails.
5. These waits and flows are not clearly surfaced in the legacy interface—they are essentially invisible speedbumps.

My Design Solutions

Redesigned User Journey:
Step User Action System Response Why It Matters
0 Arrives at Account Summary Overview of accounts Familiar hub for all banking activity.
1 Clicks “Transfer Funds” from main menu Transfer screen with visible tooltips Surfaces crucial beneficiary activation guidance before wasted effort.
2 Tooltip on transact cards: “Please ensure your beneficiary is added & active before proceeding. For new beneficiaries, full transfer limits may activate after 30min–24hrs. Go to Requests → View/Delete Beneficiaries to check status and limits.” User learns about security and waiting periods up front.
3 Follows link to “Requests” → “View/Delete Beneficiaries” Sees beneficiary status (Active/Pending), adds new beneficiary, completes OTP flow, sees countdown or next available transfer limit Ensures all necessary steps and info are handled before transaction.
4 Confirms beneficiary now “Active” and checks any limit info Proceeds to successful transfer flow Delivers an experience closer to UPI’s simplicity and instant feedback.
