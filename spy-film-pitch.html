Project Goals

The director’s initial preference was to create a PowerPoint presentation for the film pitch. However, the limitations of PowerPoint quickly became apparent: aside from the lack of interactivity, the file could also be problematic when shared with others due to missing fonts, potential formatting changes, and other technical issues. More importantly, PowerPoint wasn’t engaging enough to stand out in a competitive industry.
It wasn’t a lack of content.
The challenge was to present the film pitch in a way that felt modern, engaging, and seamless. This required solving the issue of integrating background music, which is a key part of storytelling, into the presentation in a way that played continuously without interruption.
Film Pitch Website Requirements

Create a Compelling Pitch Experience

How can we ensure the film pitch is engaging, memorable, and leaves a strong impression on the producers?

Eliminate PowerPoint Issues

Can we create a more stable and professional solution by eliminating the formatting problems and font issues commonly associated with PowerPoint presentations?

Integrate Interactive Elements

Can we incorporate interactive features that allow the producers to engage with the content more deeply, increasing their investment in the project?

Ensure Seamless Music Integration

How can we ensure the background music plays seamlessly without being interrupted, creating a more immersive and professional pitch experience?

Design Process

The project began as a PowerPoint pitch deck, but we quickly realized the limitations: static visuals, broken fonts, and no support for background music. We reframed the solution entirely: what if the pitch felt more like a cinematic experience than a slideshow?
But How can you make a pitch deck presentation immersive and compelling?
A website presented itself as the ideal solution to these issues, allowing the content to be shared universally without worrying about formatting or software incompatibilities.
Core Design Deliverables & Activities
Step 1: UX/UI and Visual Design

Conducted UX research, created wireframes and high-fidelity mockups, and crafted a visual style inspired by cinematic spy thrillers.

Step 2: Website Development

Built a responsive site using a CMS platform with custom JavaScript to control transitions, interactions, and background music.

User Journey
The user journey was crafted to mirror the structure of a cinematic experience. It began with a secure login screen, followed by a cinematic landing page that set the tone with spy-inspired visuals. From there, users encountered an interactive reveal: a plane-shaped button that, when clicked, triggered background music and unfolded the full pitch content. This final step—the full pitch experience—combined visuals, motion, and sound to deliver a dynamic and memorable alternative to static presentations.
Visual and Graphic Design
I designed visual assets such as an animated title card, spy-inspired UI elements, and film posters. These helped create a cohesive brand language that extended beyond the web experience into promotional materials.

Website Development

I created a password-protected website to present a film pitch in an interactive way. The site opened with a blank dark screen. A plane-shaped button unlocked the full pitch experience.
A simple interface that opened into a full-screen, cinematic reveal.
After login, the user saw a minimal page with one button. Clicking the button started background music and revealed the pitch content. The website was responsive and worked on all devices. Music playback was smooth and uninterrupted.
Key Interactive Features
1 Plane-shaped button

2 Fully responsive layout

Conclusion: Thoughts and Takeaways

The goal of this project was to make a digital pitch deck that, while solving for the limitations of a traditional pitch deck, was also innovative and technically sound.

A pitch deck website was a unique solution but with innovation comes new problems - here, it was the autoplay limitation on browsers. However, this problem was creatively bypassed using interactive elements that fit thematically.

Key Takeaway: UX for Creative Industries

A Pitch deck is primarily a business presentation document, and incorporating a non-traditional format allows for UX to be used in Creative industries such as Film and Advertising.

Key Takeaway: Understanding real-world technical limitations

Product solutions always seem feasible when doing research, but an understanding of the existing software/hardware limitations might help design a Product that works in the real world.
