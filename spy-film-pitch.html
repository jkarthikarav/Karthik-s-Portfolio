Project Goals

Challenges: Creating a Movie Pitch Deck PPT with Seamless Background Music

The director wanted a PowerPoint presentation with uninterrupted background music while also wanting to email them as PDFs.
However, PowerPoints/PDFs don’t support uninterrupted autoplaying nor seamless music playback.


What could be done?

A website was a viable alternative.

What could it do?

Seamless Music integration possibility
Provide a Cinematic and Immersive feel
Interactive Spy-themed UI elements.

However, there is a technical Constraint that is still solvable.

Most browsers block audio autoplay unless the user interacts with the page.

Website Development

  • The website was password protected and could only be accessed through Login.
  • After login, the web page appears with only one single visible section; the rest of the website is rendered fully in darkness.
  • This section would contain looped footage of a WWII era black and white/sepia toned video with a Plane-shaped button in the center with the label "Debrief".
  • On clicking the plane button: The darkened sections of the website are revealed, with a force-scroll down to an animated video that plays the Title card as well as the movie’s one-liner. This also triggers the music.

Conclusion: Thoughts and Takeaways

The goal of this project was to make a digital pitch deck that while solving for the limitations of a traditional pitch deck, was also innovative and technically sound.

​ A pitch deck website was a unique solution but with innovation comes new problems - here, it was the autoplay limitation on browsers. However, this problem was creatively bypassed using interactive elements that fit thematically.

Key Takeaway: UX for Creative Industries

A Pitch deck is primarily a business presentation document, and incorporating a non-traditional format allows for UX to be used in Creative industries such as Film and Advertising.

Key Takeaway: Understanding real-world technical limitations

Product solutions always seem feasible when doing research, but an understanding of the existing software/hardware limitations might help design a Product that works in the real world.
