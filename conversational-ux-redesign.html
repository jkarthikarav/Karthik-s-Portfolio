Project Goals

This project focuses on improving the user experience of Buoy Health's AI chatbot through a conversational UX audit and flow redesign. The goal was to identify usability issues and enhance the efficiency of symptom selection, particularly addressing a problem where editing a symptom isn't allowed and deleting one clears the list - this breaks three of Neilson's Heuristics (User Control and Freedom, Recognition Rather Than Recall, Help Users Recognize, Diagnose, and Recover from Errors).

By examining the chatbotâ€™s interaction flow in a self-paced solo product walkthrough (while nursing a minor medical issue used during the AI Chatbot's conversation), I propose solutions that would allow users to remove individual symptoms without affecting the rest of the flow, thus improving overall usability and efficiency.

User Research & Discovery

To understand how users interact with Buoy Health's AI chatbot, I conducted a product walkthrough to identify key usability issues. This quick evaluation allowed me to pinpoint areas of improvement in the symptom selection process and gain insights into user pain points, which directly informed the redesign and flow adjustments.

Design Suggestions

Issue: Here, Users cannot remove a single symptom (e.g., "headache") without causing the deletion of the entire list, and there's no option to directly edit symptoms without starting aain at the symptoms step.

Solution 01: Adds an Edit button next to Remove on each symptom card. This allows to adjust inputs without clearing all previous selections.

Solution 02: Supports individual symptom removal, resolving the issue of forced deletion and improving control mid-conversation.

Conclusion: Thoughts and Takeaways

This audit demonstrates how a brief product walkthrough can reveal critical UX issues in AI-driven Health tools. During my walkthrough of the chatbot, I identified several issues, such as inconsistencies in editing and deleting an input. These problems went beyond visual design and will impact user trust as well as user experience.

I proposed solutions to address these concerns by adjusting the symptom process, providing useful feedback and better control, aimed at making more understandable interactions.

Key Takeaway: Small Changes, Big Impact

A simple audit of the user experience can uncover major friction points.
